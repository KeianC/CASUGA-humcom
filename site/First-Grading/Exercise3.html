Declassified Groom Survival Guide

A quick way to set your pet, during Groom-day

by: Keian Casuga

Ever had those moments where your dog looks like utter mess and it's very difficult for you to just try and clean that? Do you have moments where you just want to
cut the ever living crap out of it? Taking your doggo to a groomer can help you keep your dog clean, healthy, and comfortable, but sometimes we can just be
extremely cheap to pay for something as exclusive as that. Well, with the Declassified Groom Survival guide. It can help you style and groom your pupper into
something snazzy without wasting a dime. All you need is the required equipments

Ordered list exmaples (but bullets)

  • Scissors
  • Towel
  • Warm Water
  • Dog Shampoo
  • Soap
  • Hair dryer
  • (Optional)Nail clippers

Step 1

Trim some floof

First things first, we cut some of the hair, Bathing the doggo with that much hair can be a huge hassle, some necessary cuts are intended in order for the grooming
to go smoothly not only for you, but also for your dog. Trust me, You're gonna thank me later

Step 2

Bathe the floof

Now that some hair is out of the way, You need to bathe the doggo first or else it can easily ruin the hair of the doggo and not to mention you might hurt the fella, A great way
to start off is by bathing the doggo first, that way you can remove the excess dirts by using Water and Soap.

Step 3

Dry'em

Finally, the doggo is now squeeky clean, now to dry'em, Not only can this help you save some time, but this will help you avoid the hair to be clumped up together. Use Some
towel. Blow dry'em, brush it according to the direction of his hair, use a comb if u have to detangle some hair, but a soft brush for the rest. and comfort them, treat
them after groom to make them more comfortable.

Step 4

Cutting time

After giving the doggo sometime to rest and relaxation, now its time for the real thing. Be extremely careful if you use scissors to avoid injuring yourself or your pet. Try to
cut parallel to the growth of the hair to avoid a choppy look.

Next thing is to Clear out the doggo's eyes. A healthy eye should be clear and should not show any signs of >irritation or unusual discharge. Don't try to trim hair away from the eyes
yourself, as you might injure your pet. Ask your vet or groomer to do it for you.

Afterwards, the doggo's ear. It's normal for a clean ear to have some wax in it, but there shouldn't be any particular smell to it. To clean your dog's ears, apply some ear cleaning
solution Wipe dirt and wax away from the inner ear, but don't rub vigorously, as this might cause sores. Don't push too far into the ear, either. If your dog has drop ears like
a basset hound, wipe the inside of the ear flag as dirt collects there as well. The groomers rule of thumb is to clean only what you can see.
